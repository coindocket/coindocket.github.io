Frequently Asked Questions

How much are the fees?

1.0% for escrow transactions, and a yet to be determined fee for the creation of a digital document into the bitcoin blockchain and executing the terms of an agreement. As the value of a single bitcoin increases we will lower fees to account for the offset in rising bitcoin valuation, which will still be cheaper than many traditional options available today.

I thought Bitcoin had escrow built in?

Bitcoin allows two parties to transact in a trustless manner without a third party. What is not available today is an easy way to create a 2-of-3 transaction, transaction terms that can be enforced, and ultimately what happens when a transaction does not go as planned. We are building the platform today that will enable easy to use consumer facing applications for the blockchain ecosystem.

Why would I use Coindocket?

Frankly you don't have too, and thats the beauty of bitcoin and the freedom it empowers it's users with. No longer do you have to rely on a centralized organization to handle a transaction, however the benefit to you the user and the community overall is an simple user interface to facilitate escrow transactions, decentralized arbitration, and drag-n-drop smart agreements between two untrusted parties.

How are disputes handled?

In the event that a asmart agreement does not finalize, the mediation process will be handled by the network of global agents who will act as independent contractors and can set their own fees for arbitration. By utilizing a network of experts across the globe you can use language specific and geographical relevant agents who can best handle all aspects of mediation.
